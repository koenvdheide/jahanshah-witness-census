# Yınanç, "CİHAN-ŞAH": MEB İslâm Ansiklopedisi C. III (1945)

**Source**: Mükrimin H. Yınanç, "Cihan-Şah", *İslâm Ansiklopedisi*, Cilt III, İstanbul: MEB Yayınları, 1945, pp. 173–189 (entry runs PDF pages 89–96 of the Internet Archive scan, printed-page numbers 178–189).
**Access**: Internet Archive, `meb-islam-ansiklopedisi_202308`, Cilt 03 PDF (49.6 MB, downloaded 2026-05-02).
**OCR quality**: Mediocre: scanned-text PDF with frequent character substitutions (q→g, ‹→ş, etc.), but key passages legible. Quotations below have been corrected only where OCR garbling is unambiguous (e.g. "Çiban-Şah" → "Cihan-Şah").
**Extraction date**: 2026-05-02 (the tezkire sweep).

## Status as a tezkire-adjacent source

Yınanç 1945 is a modern *encyclopaedia* entry, not a primary tezkire: but it is the foundational Turkish-language synthesis predating Minorsky 1954 and is the source most heavily cited by Macit, TEIS Yesevi, and TDV İslam Ansiklopedisi. It also catalogues the primary Persian-language tezkires that mention Cihanşah.

## Sample verses quoted

**Yınanç quotes NO sample verses from Hakîkî's Divan.** His treatment is purely political-historical and bibliographic. The poetry section is summarised in a single paragraph (see below) without textual reproduction.

## The Hakîkî / poetry paragraph (PDF p. 96, printed p. 188)

Verbatim transcription of the OCR (Yınanç's prose, partially garbled but sense-clear):

> [Cihan-Şah,] memleketinin muhtelif yerlerini âbideler ile süsleyen bu hükümdar **arap şiirinin büs-bütün çöktüğü ve İran şiirinin de inbitata başladığı bu asırda şâirleri himâye etmiş ve kendisi de Hakiki mahlâsı ile farisi ve türkçe şiirler yazmış ve bir divan vücuda getirmişti** (bk. mad. AZERİ).
>
> XVI. asır sonunda eserlerini yazmış olan Kadı Nūr-Allah al-Mar'aşi (*Macālis al-mu'minīn*, Paris, Bibl. Nat. Suppl. Pers., nr. 190 ve 301; Esad Efendi kütüp., nr. 1280), bu sülâlenin bütün hükümdarlarını şi'i addetmekte ve Cihan-Şah'ı da onlar arasında saymaktadır. Fakat kardeşi ve Bagdad hükümdarı İspan hakkında mevcut olan ve doğruluğu tesbit edilemeyen bir isnadın Cihan-Şah'a da teşmil edilmesi hiç bir esasa dayanmamaktadır.

**Critical observation**: Yınanç's only literary characterization is that Cihanşah "wrote Persian and Turkish poems under the pen-name Hakîkî and produced a divan": he refers the reader to the *Azeri* article (Köprülü 1942) for further treatment.

## Tezkires/biographical-dictionary sources cited by Yınanç

### Persian / Arabic tezkires named in the bibliography

- **Devletşâh, Tazkirat al-shu'arā**: Yınanç cites the Leyden 1901 edition, p. 554 (= Browne edition). This is the primary Cihanşah-contemporary tezkire.
- **Kadı Nūr-Allah al-Mar'aşi, Majālis al-mu'minīn**: late 16th-century Safavid Shi'i tezkire/biographical dictionary; Yınanç cites BnF Suppl. Pers. 190, BnF Suppl. Pers. 301, Esad Efendi 1280. **Mar'aşi includes Cihanşah among the Qaraqoyunlu rulers he labels as Shi'i**: Yınanç rebuts this attribution.
- **'Abd al-Razzāq Samarqandī, Maṭlaʿ al-saʿdayn** (BnF Suppl. Pers. + Esad Efendi 2125 + Edirne Sultan Selim 1492): political chronicle; emphasises Cihanşah's "adâlet ve imârcılık" (justice and patronage of building).
- **Mīrkhwānd, Rawżat al-ṣafā** (Bombay 1293 ed.), VI, pp. 3–400: chronicle.
- **Ḥāfiẓ-i Abrū, Zubdat al-tavārīkh** (Fatih 4371): chronicle.
- **Abū Bakr-i Ṭihrānī, Kitāb-i Diyārbakriyya**: Aq Qoyunlu-side chronicle.
- **Ḥasan Beg Rūmlū, Aḥsan al-tavārīkh** (Nuruosmaniye 3317).
- **Ibn Taghrībirdī, al-Manhal al-ṣāfī** (Nuruosmaniye 3428, entry CİHAN-ŞAH); *Ḥawādiṯ al-duhūr* (California 1932 ed.); *al-Nujūm al-zāhira* VII (Ayasofya 3499).
- **Sakhāwī, al-Tibr al-masbūk** (Bulak 1869); *al-Ḍawʾ al-lāmiʿ* (Mısır 1334), III, p. 80.
- **Ġiyāṯī, Tārīkh al-Ġiyāṯī**: Arabic chronicle.
- **Zakaria** (17th-c. Armenian historian): Brosset, *Collection d'historiens arméniens*, I, 3–5.
- **Haydar Râzi, Macma' al-tavârih** (BnF Suppl. Pers. 1330), I, var. 227: Yınanç cites this for the famous Uzun Hasan saying upon Pir-Budak's death: «"İki idi bir oldu, igit idi pir oldu"» (variant: "Two became one, the youth became old").

### Manuscript-witnesses-of-Cihanşah's-letters cited (NOT poetry witnesses)

- BnF Bibl. Nat. Suppl. Pers., var. 161–171, 208–209: Arabic majmuʿa containing 5 letters from Cihanşah/Pir Budak to Mamluk sultans (859, 861, undated)
- BnF Bibl. Nat. Suppl. Pers. 1815, var. 78ff., 163–165: 2 letters from Sultan Abu Sa'id Gürgan to Cihanşah
- Esad Efendi 333, var. 43–45: Münşaât collection
- **Esad Efendi 3340: vakfiye (deed of pious endowment) by Cihanşah for Tabriz hayrat (charitable foundations): VERIFIED in TDV İslam Ansiklopedisi** (Konukçu 1993); Yınanç also notes Arşiv kılavuzu has a photograph of one such vakfiye
- Esad Efendi 3673, Husâmeddin-zâde mecmuası, var. 59ff.: Cihanşah's long reply
- Nuruosmaniye 4981: mecmuʿa with copies of Cihanşah letters
- Feridun Bey, *Münşaât* (İstanbul 1274), I, pp. 166–173, 223–273: printed letters

## Bibliographical implications

- **Devletşâh's tezkire is the primary 1487 source** quoted by all later Turkish synthesizers (TEIS, Macit, TDV) for the "70 years" lifespan datum. The text on PDF p. 96 (printed p. 188) is too OCR-garbled to confirm whether Yınanç's bibliography reproduces sample verses, but cross-checking against Macit 2000 confirms that Devletşâh's entry is purely biographical-political; he does NOT quote sample ghazals from Cihanşah.

- **Kadı Nūr-Allah Mar'aşi's *Majālis al-muʾminīn*** is the *most theologically interesting* tezkire-adjacent source: it explicitly classes Cihanşah as Shi'i. This bears on the religious context of his Hurufi/Hallâcî mysticism in his ghazals (Macit 2000 p. 14 reads the closing of Gazel 1: "Mansur oldı der-meydân-ı ışk": as Hallacian).

- **No paper / decoration / illumination details for any witness are given in Yınanç 1945**: confirming the broader pattern that pre-Minorsky Turkish encyclopaedia-tezkire sources do not engage codicology.
