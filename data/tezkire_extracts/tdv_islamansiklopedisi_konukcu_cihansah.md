## Status

Modern Turkish encyclopaedia entry. The successor article to Yınanç 1945. **Not a primary tezkire**, but it explicitly cites tezkire-derived information.

## Sample verses quoted

**ZERO sample verses.** direct HTTP fetch reports: «The entry states Cihan Şah "wrote Turkish and Persian poems under the pen name Hakîkî" but **does not quote any sample verses**. It notes that V. Minorsky examined his poetry and provided examples in an article.»

The poetry collection comprised "105 Persian, eighty-seven Turkish gazels and thirty-two Turkish rubāʿīs": these are the **Minorsky 1954 numbers**, NOT Macit 2000's revised counts (113 Persian / 92 Turkish / 33 tuyughs).

## Manuscript witnesses cited

**One reference, NOT to a Divan witness but to a vakfiye (waqf deed)**:

> **Süleymaniye Library, Esad Efendi collection, no. 3340**: described as containing "copies of the waqf documents concerning Cihan Şah's charitable foundations in Tabriz."

This corroborates Yınanç 1945's bibliography (which already cited Esad Efendi 3340 for Cihanşah's vakfiye for Tebriz hayrat). It is a **prose / archival witness, not a poetry witness**: but it is the most-likely-decorated manuscript object connected to Cihanşah's name in any Turkish collection (vakfiyes were often calligraphically and decoratively elaborate). **unresolved question: an authenticated YEK portal future access work needs to photograph Esad Efendi 3340 if it has been digitised.**

## Tezkire/biographical-source references

The entry lists 13 sources, including:

- **Ebû Bekr-i Tihrânī** (= Abū Bakr-i Ṭihrānī), *Kitāb-i Diyārbakriyya*
- **Sehāvī** (= Sakhāwī), *al-Tibr al-masbūk* / *al-Ḍawʾ al-lāmiʿ*
- **Feridun Bey**, *Münşaât*
- **Uzunçarşılı**, *Anadolu Beylikleri ve Akkoyunlu, Karakoyunlu Devletleri* (1969)
- **Vladimir Minorsky**, "Kara Koyunlu Cihan Şah ve Şiirleri" (Mine Erol trans.), *Selçuklu Araştırmaları Dergisi* II, Ankara 1970, pp. 153–180

The Minorsky 1970 Turkish translation is the key secondary work cited. **No primary tezkire (Devletşâh, Sam Mirza, Fakhri-i Heravi, Atashkada, Riyāḍ al-ʿĀrifīn) is explicitly cited in the TDV entry text**: Konukçu treats Cihanşah purely as ruler, not as poet.
