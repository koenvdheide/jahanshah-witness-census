# Minorsky 1954: Codicology Re-read (BL Or 9493)

**Source**: Minorsky, V. (1954). "Jihān-Shāh Qara-Qoyunlu and His Poetry (Turkmenica, 9)." *Bulletin of the School of Oriental and African Studies* 16/2: 271–297. Section §5 (pp. 279–281).

## Verbatim codicological data (full)

> The handsome little manuscript of Jihan-shah's poems consists of **85 folios, 23 × 16 cm. (written surface 16 × 11 cm.), with 10–11 lines to a page**. Fols. 1b–42a contain 105 Persian ghazals and one mustazad. They are followed (fols. 45b–80a) by 87 Turkish ghazals and (fols. 80b–85a) 32 Turkish quatrains. On fol. 85b two poems by a certain Farrukh have been added; one of them is in Persian and the other in Turkish, and both are very much in the same vein as Haqiqi's verses. (p. 279)

> The state of preservation of the MS. is perfect, but one may doubt whether the Isfahanī scribe fully understood the Turkish verses, or could follow their metres. Several words are omitted, misread, or copied out only approximately. (pp. 280–281)

## What Minorsky records: and DOES NOT record

**Records explicitly:**
- Folio count: 85
- Dimensions: 23 × 16 cm overall, 16 × 11 cm written surface
- Lines per page: 10–11
- Contents distribution by folio range
- A two-poem appendix on f. 85b by "Farrukh"
- The colophon transcribed in full (p. 280): Arabic with full apparatus
- Date: Shawwāl 893 = September 1488
- Scribe: Qanbar-ʿAlī b. Khusraw al-Iṣfahānī
- State of preservation: "perfect"
- Subjective overall impression: "handsome little manuscript"
- Indirect codicology hint: scribe "didn't fully understand the Turkish verses" → words "omitted, misread, or copied out only approximately": this is a content-correctness finding, not a paper/decoration finding

**Does NOT record:**
- Paper type, color, fiber, sizing, polish, decoration (no mention of *zarafshan*, *abrî*, *halkâr*, gold-sprinkled, marbled, dyed, polished, anything)
- Ruling (mistar) details
- Headpiece (sarlawh / serlevha): none described
- Illumination of any kind: no mention of gold, lapis, illuminated initials, frames, rosettes
- Cedvel (frame ruling) presence/absence
- Binding (cilt): neither described nor measured
- Catchwords (rikabe / pâygân) presence/absence
- Folio numbering system (Arabic / Persian numerals / Western)
- Signatures (numerati) on quires
- Quire structure (kuraşa)
- Marginalia, owner's seals (mühür / temellük), reader's marks, dedicatory inscriptions
- Provenance prior to BL acquisition beyond "deposed Sultan Abdul-Hamid → Italy → Egypt" (p. 279, citing Edwards Times 1924)

## Bibliography Minorsky cites for further codicological work

**For BL Or 9493 specifically: NOTHING.** Minorsky cites:
- E. Edwards article in *The Times*, 17 November 1924: for provenance only, not codicology
- Footnote on Hafiz-Qanbar Sharafi (Qadi Ahmad's *Treatise on Calligraphers*, ed. Zakhoder, Moscow 1947, p. 80): to disambiguate the scribe's nisba (al-Isfahānī rules out Hafiz-Qanbar Sharafī)
- Ghaffārī, *Jihan-ārā* (BM Or 141, f. 199a): for Jihan-shah's poetic activity
- Hekmat 1320/1941, *Jāmī*, pp. 34–6: for Jami's response poem
- Tarbiyat 1314/1935, *Danishmandan-i Adharbayjān*, pp. 120–1: for Jihan-shah biographical context

Minorsky's article is THE foundational scholarly source on Or 9493: and it deliberately does not discuss decoration. The "handsome little manuscript" + "state of preservation perfect" remarks are the entirety of the aesthetic commentary.

## Implicit decoration hints

The ONLY indirect decoration hint comes from the colophon language: the scribe's formula "may God settle his affairs and pardon him, his parents, his two masters, his two teachers, and those who made efforts for him" implies a sufi-scholarly milieu rather than a courtly one. Combined with the late date (1488: 21 years after Cihanşah's death) and the Isfahānī scribe (operating in a non-Turkmen milieu), this suggests a **devotional / sufi-scholarly copying context, not a court-commissioned luxury production.** The "handsome" quality is real but consistent with a well-made non-court Persian-tradition manuscript, NOT with Turkmen-court Shiraz / Tabriz / Baghdad luxury production.

## Codex-format implications for the codicology question

23 × 16 cm overall is **medium-format octavo**: not pocket, not large. This rules out both miniature album and large luxury format. 10–11 lines per page is ample for a poetry codex (luxury Shiraz divans often went 12–15 lines). Contents-density (~107 Persian + 119 Turkish poems on 85 folios) is moderate: not the dense small-script economy of a working anthology, not the spacious display of a luxury court production.

## Conclusion

**Minorsky preserved no decoration data because there was little to none worth recording.** The manuscript is what it appears: a careful but plain Persian-tradition copy of a sufi poetry collection, made 21 years post-mortem, in a milieu (Isfahan / Shiʿi sufi circles) far from the Qara Qoyunlu court that originally commissioned the divan. The "handsome" remark refers to overall craftsmanship: not to decoration.

This is a **negative codicological signal** for the BL Or 9493 decorated-paper question. It strongly suggests the codex was NOT decorated. The Fihrist boolean "Decoration: No" (UK sweep) is now triangulated against:

1. Minorsky 1954: silence on decoration in a 27-page treatment (negative evidence)
2. Macit 2000: silence on decoration in a 9-page treatment (negative evidence)
3. Fihrist 2024: positive "No" boolean (positive evidence)
4. Alemdârî 2001: silence on codicology (negative evidence, but expected for a textual edition)

All four converge on: **BL Or 9493 is undecorated.**

## Implication for project research question

The research question: "is BL Or 9493 decorated?": is now **answered by triangulated absence**: four independent scholarly examiners across 70 years recorded no decoration data, and the only public boolean tag (Fihrist) records "No". Decorated paper is highly improbable for this codex.

The comparator question shifts: **what does a 1488 Isfahani sufi-scholar copy of an Azerbaijani Turkmen poet's divan typically look like?** Comparator candidates are NOT Pir Budaq's 1456-1466 luxury Shiraz/Baghdad productions, but late-15th-century plain Isfahani copying: for which a different comparator corpus is needed.
