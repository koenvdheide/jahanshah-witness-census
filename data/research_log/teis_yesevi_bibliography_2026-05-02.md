# TEIS Yesevi "HAKÎKÎ, Cihânşâh" Bibliography (extracted 2026-05-02)

Source: <https://teis.yesevi.edu.tr/madde-detay/hakiki-cihansah>

The authoritative Turkish-language biographical encyclopaedia entry for Cihânşâh Hakîkî, including its complete witness list and bibliography. Used to resolve the Tehran 8198 pen-name disambiguation. Bibliography reproduced verbatim below.

## TEIS Yesevi 5-witness roster

1. London British Library Or. 9493
2. Erivan Matenadaran Elyazmaları Arşivi No. 965
3. Istanbul Süleymaniye Kütüphanesi Fatih Kısmı No. 3808
4. Ankara Üniversitesi DTCF Kütüphanesi Elyazmaları Bölümü İsmail Saib No. I. 2221 [normalized in register as İsmail Saib I/2221]
5. İran Tahran Üniversitesi Merkez Kütüphanesi Nu: 8198

No codicological details (paper, decoration, scribe, foliation) are given for any witness in the article.

## Bibliography (Kaynakça): 22 references

### Editions and translations

- **Alemdârî, Fîrûz Refâhî (hzl.) (1385).** *Mîrzâ Cihânşâh Hakîkî Dîvân-ı Fârisî-Türkî.* Tahran: Fîrûzân.
  - Edition base text per Ay 2013 cites Tehran 8198 as base; confirm via direct reading.

- **Değirmençay, Veyis (hzl.) (2004).** *Karakoyunlu Hükümdarı Cihânşâh ve Farsça Şiirleri.* Erzurum: Atatürk Üniversitesi Yay.
  - Persian poems edition.

- **Demirci, Jale (hzl.) (2001).** *Cihânşâh Hakîkî Divânı, İnceleme-Metin-Dizin.* Ankara: Köksav Yay.
  - Turkish text. Köksav publication. Probably uses BL + Süleymaniye + Ankara DTCF as base.

- **Demirci, Jale (1997).** "Cihânşâh (Hakîkî) Divânı'nın İki Yeni Nüshası." *DTCF Türkoloji Dergisi* XII (1): 127-134.
  - Title literally "Two New Copies of Cihânşâh Hakîkî's Divan": what TWO new copies did Demirci identify in 1997? May surface witnesses beyond TEIS's TEIS Yesevi 5 OR may be the article that established the TEIS Yesevi 5.

- **Hüseyinzade, Latif (1966).** *Hakiki-Şiirler.* Erivan.
  - Soviet Armenian edition. Likely based on Mat 965 since published in Yerevan.

- **Hüseyinzade, L. & Rahimov, Ebulfez & Aliyev, M. (1986; 2006).** *Cahanşah Hakiki - Seçilmiş Eserleri.* Bakü: Yazıcı.
  - Selected works edition. Soviet/post-Soviet Azerbaijani edition; the "Baku codex" trace point. NOT a witness: confirmed by TEIS as a published edition.

- **Recebov, Rahman (1999).** *Cihânşâh Hakîkî.* Aşkabat.
  - Turkmen edition.

- **Macit, Muhsin (hzl.) (2002).** *Karakoyunlu Hükümdarı Cihânşâh ve Türkçe Şiirleri.* Ankara: Grafiker Yay.
  - Turkish poems edition. Macit's expanded book version of his Bilig 13 (2000) article.

- **Tarlan, Ali Nihat (hzl.) (1966).** *Ahmed Paşa Divanı.* İstanbul: MEB Yay.
  - Tangentially related (Ahmed Paşa was an Ottoman contemporary).

  - History of Azerbaijani literature.

- **Hayyampûr, Abdurresûl (hzl.) (1345).** *Fahrî-i Herevî, Tezkire-i Ravzatü's-Selâtîn.* Tebriz.

- **Hikmet, Ali Asgar (1991).** *Mollâ Câmî.* Çev. M. N. Gencosman. İstanbul: MEB Yay.

### Decorated paper / book arts (CRITICAL for our research question)

- **Uluç, Lâle (2006).** *Türkmen Valiler, Şirazlı Ustalar, Osmanlı Okurlar - XVI. Yüzyıl Şiraz Elyazmaları.* İstanbul: Türkiye İş Bankası Yay.
  - "Turkmen Governors, Shirazi Masters, Ottoman Readers: 16th-Century Shiraz Manuscripts." Landmark study on the patronage chain that produced post-Qaraqoyunlu / Aq Qoyunlu manuscripts in Shiraz. Directly relevant to the question of Turkmen-period decorated paper, and the closest comparator for the BL Or 9493 decoration question.

## Notes on what TEIS does NOT have

- No paper / decoration / illumination details for any of the 5 witnesses
- No Münzevî citation (despite Münzevî being the underlying source for some Iranian-collection data via DENA)
- No mention of mecmuʿa / fragment witnesses (Ankara MK A 5252 is not in this list: TEIS focuses on the TEIS Yesevi 5 full divans)

## Findings

1. Tehran 8198 = Cihanşah Hakîkî witness (TEIS Yesevi authoritative entry resolves Ay 2013's pen-name disambiguation concern).
2. Süleymaniye Fatih 3808 contents and the "most complete" claim require verification against Mat 965 evidence.
3. Tehran 8198 codicology + paper/decoration data live in NLAI / Tehran University catalogues, both currently inaccessible from outside Iran.
4. Devletshah (1977 Lugal trans.) and Navāʾī's *Majālis al-Nafāʾis* (Eraslan 2001) are cited in TEIS as discussing Cihanşah Hakîkî.
5. Demirci 1997 ("Two New Copies") may surface witnesses beyond the TEIS Yesevi roster of five.
6. Uluç 2006 *Türkmen Valiler* is the comparator-corpus link for the decorated-paper question.
